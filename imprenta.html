LA IMPRENTA DE LOS PUEBLOS

activism
typographic intervention

Posters have been great political and social protagonists throughout history. They also are one of the design pieces that cause me most fascination because they were in my point of view, the first and clearest intersection between design and activism in history. Although used mostly for commercial purposes, nowadays they continue to play an important role of protest and denouncement in public space.

Argentine journalist and thinker Rodolfo Walsh put it so well “Las paredes son la imprenta de los pueblos” Walls are the printing press of the people while complaining against the brutalities of the Argentine dictatorship. This gallery contains a selection of posters I have designed individually and in collaboration with other collectives in the last 8 years in which I have studied the aestheticization of politics and protest, and explored analog and digital techniques for both design and reproduction.
