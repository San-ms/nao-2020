WELCOME!

activism
typographic installation

The Port of Palermo dates back more than 2,700 years. Nowadays it serves over two million passengers and around five million tonnes of cargo every year. Its privileged position in the Mediterranean sea serves as a lively bridge between the Middle East, north Africa and Europe. Nonetheless here in Italy like in order parts of Europe, not all travelers are welcomed. Refugee seeking travelers are sent back home or imprisoned in refugee camps as soon as they manage to get to these coast lines.

This typographic installation was made with several stretches of “do-not-cross” tape found in the Palermo’s Stazione Marittima port, the place were most “legal” travelers enter the island on board huge cruise ships and boats. The lengths of tape were set up nearby, facing the sea in the Foro Italico coast line, a famous touristic passage. The most interesting thing was discussing with the people that came by while making the intervention. Who is really welcomed and how? What can we do as citizens, businessmen and women, or artist to address this aberration? Feeling curious and eager to help? consider getting in touch with your local migrant support group.
