Palermo Port dates back to over 2,700 years ago. Nowadays it serves over two million passengers and around five million tonnes of cargo every year. The privileged position of it in the mediterranean sea serves as a lively bridge between middle east, north Africa and Europe. Though here, like in order parts of Europe, not all travelers are welcomed. Refugee seeking travelers are sent back home or imprisoned in  refugee camps when they get to these coast lines. This typographic installation was made with several pieces of “do-not-cross” band found in the Palermo’s Stazione Marittima port, and assembled together nearby in the Foro Italico coast line. Feeling curious? Wanna be part of the solution? Contsider donating or getting involved with your migrant support local group.
