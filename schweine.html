SCHWEINE-GLÜCK

editorial
information design

In german Schweineglück* literally means pig’s luck but also having a really f*cking good luck! Also in Germany around 56.415.489 pigs are killed every year, the equivalent to 8,226 football fields full of standing pigs. One third of this production will end in the trash mainly because it is cheaply overproduced. Still, pigs are very much appreciated in our pop-culture. Perhaps because they are incredibly intelligent and loving animals, and besides because we humans share a lot of biological and social characteristics with them.

Claiming to love animals while producing, killing, eating and wasting them at the same time is the great irony that gave life to this project. In this coloring book the sweet pigs we all love from cartoons and movies, tell us the story about how it is to be born and raised in a mass production farm, to be slaughtered in a high-tech slaughtering complex, and end up being sold as cheap meat, so cheap no one will buy or eat it.

This project has been presented in different contexts were people are encouraged to color it, like in “täglich geöffnet” an exhibition of projects from students of the Burg Giebichenstein Kunsthochschule, dedicated to daily life matters. It is amazing to see how much kids liked it, and how willing they were to share their thoughts on empathy and compassion for the little pigs.

*This project was developed during the Information Design class with Prof. Matthias Görlich in the Burg Kunsthochschule Halle 2017.
