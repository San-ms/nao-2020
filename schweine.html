56.415.489 pigs are killed every year in Germany, the equivalent to 8,226 football fields full of standing pigs. One third of this production will end in the trash, mainly because it is cheaply overproduced. Still, pigs are very much appreciated in our (pop) culture. Perhaps because they are incredible intelligent and loving animals, and besides we humans share a lot of biological and social characteristics with them.

Claiming to love animals while producing, killing, eating and wasting them at the same time is the great irony that gave life into this project. In this coloring book the sweet pigs we all love from cartoons and movies, tell us the story about how it is to be born and raised in a mass production farm, to be slaughter in a high-tech slaughtering complex, and end up being sold as cheap meat, so cheap no one will buy or eat it.

This project has been presented in different exhibitions were people are encouraged to color it. It is amazing to see how much kids liked it, and how willing the were to share their thoughts on empathy and compassion for the little pigs.


* In german schweineglück means literally pig’s luck but also having a really f*cking good luck!
