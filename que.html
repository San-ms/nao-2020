¿QUE? / YO NO TRABAJO

activism
typographic intervention

As students of the Burg Kunsthochschule Halle* (GER), we got the opportunity to go to Cuba in an exchange program with students of the ISDI Instituto Superior de Diseño in Havana. During two weeks we were set to research the conditions, challenges and creative ways of contemporary cuban graphic designers. Cuban graphic design is well known for its contribution to the revolución in the fifties but, how do cuban graphic designers work today in this island that’s slowly opening to the world? Out of the exchange with our cuban fellow students an d after interviewing a group graphic designers we organized and co-created the exhibition YO NO TRABAJO (I do not work) in the cultural venue Fábrica de Arte Cubano. In this event we presented the results of our research and discussed the challenges that we, designers and design students, happen to share in both cultures.

Despite the self-evident differences between Halle (GER) and Havana (CU), we found similar concerns that go beyond the cultural borders not only in the design-praxis but also in daily life. The book ¿QUÉ? is a compilation on articles, visual essays and short stories that arise questions on sexism, personal motivations, (the privilege of) traveling, and the use (and ownership) of the internet among others. Although the topics were approached personally, the concept, design and production of this publication was a collaborative work between the Burg students back in Halle.


*Students of the Editorial Design class by Professor Anna Berckenbusch and Sanna Schiffler in Burg Kunsthochschule Halle: Luise Menz, Lena Würshing, Sandra Méndez, Katharina Peters, Sophie Pischel, Laura Martinez and Nils Krüger. Students from the ISDI: Gabriel González, José Menéndez, José Luis Cruz, Lorena Monteagudo, Laura Rojas, Aristides Torres, Pedro Rodríguez.
