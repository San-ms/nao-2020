Miss Papaya began as a research on visual culture and sexualized violence in the Colombian context*.

It is believed that around 3,794 beauty contests are held in Colombia each year most of them to promote food and agricultural goods. If we understand violence as the denial of the subjectivity of an individual, in this case of women, then the objectification of which they are victims in beauty pageants is a type of symbolic and psychological violence that dresses up in glamour and jewelry, and ends up paving the way to a culture in which other types of explicit sexual violence are normalized. The installation has two components “Reinitas" “Misses", a series of photographs that explore the aesthetization of this kind of violence. The second part “campo de batalla” "Battlefield" is an archive with a collection of press articles, essays, academic texts and research, that problematize beauty contests by placing them within the continuum of sexualized violence in the Colombian context. The project is intended to be presented in an installation and will also be hosted on a digital platform.

*The first part of the project was developed in the seminar Violent images with Prof. Eva Leithoff Burg Giebichenstein Kunsthochschule Halle 2019.
